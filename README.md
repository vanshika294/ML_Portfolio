## 🌱 My ML(Machine Learning) Learning Journey

This space documents my journey of learning Machine Learning slowly and intentionally.

I’m not starting with polished projects — I’m starting with questions, small experiments, and trying to understand things step by step. A lot of the time, things don’t make sense immediately, and there’s some uncertainty — but instead of rushing past that, I’m learning to stay with it and work through it patiently.

Right now, my focus is on understanding how models behave, why results change, and what’s actually happening behind the scenes. The path isn’t always clear — especially when it comes to knowing what actually counts as progress and skill-building.

This is less about having everything figured out and more about gradually building clarity, intuition, and confidence over time.


🌟*Every notebook, experiment, and note here is a step in understanding — not just coding. My goal is to grow steadily, question deeply, and build intuition that lasts beyond tutorials. This is a record of learning, exploring, and moving forward with purpose.*

- To learn Machine Learning properly from the ground up

## ✨ Philosophy
- I’m learning by actually doing things, not just consuming content. Writing code, testing ideas, and seeing outcomes helps things make sense in a way theory alone doesn’t.
- I don’t expect things to work perfectly. When results don’t make sense, I try to sit with that confusion instead of skipping it — that’s usually where real understanding comes from.
- I try to focus on the “why” behind everything. Even if it takes longer, understanding why a model behaves a certain way feels more valuable than just getting the “right output”.
- I’m okay with going slow and building step by step. Right now, that means working a lot with synthetic data to understand how different parts of ML actually behave.
- I don’t see small experiments as “less important”. Even simple setups can reveal a lot if you pay attention to what’s happening.
- I’m learning to be more observant — noticing patterns, changes in outputs, and how small tweaks affect results. That’s helping me build intuition gradually.
- Writing things down — explanations, notes, markdown — is a big part of my process. It forces me to think clearly and not just assume I understand something.
- I’m trying to stay consistent and not rush into things just to “tick boxes”. The goal is to build a strong foundation that I can rely on later.
- This is an evolving process. Some things are clear, many things are not yet — and I’m okay with that. The focus is on progress, not perfection.

- I’m not trying to move fast — I’m trying to understand deeply and build something that lasts.
